KASARAGOD


The district is bordered to the south by Kerala's Kannur district, and to the southeast and north by Karnataka's districts of Kodagu and Dakshina Kannada respectively. To the east, the district is bordered by the Western Ghats, while along the west, it is bordered by the Laccadive Sea. The district, covering an area of around 1992 km2, has a population (2011 census) of 1,307,375. It has four taluks, namely, Kasaragod, Hosdurg, Vellarikundu and Manjeshwaram Taluk. It has three municipalities: Kasaragod, Kanhangad, Nileshwaram and 38 Gram panchayats. Before the formation of Kerala, Kasaragod was a part of South Canara district of erstwhile Madras Presidency. Kasaragod became part of Kannur district following the reorganisation of states and formation of Kerala in 1 November 1956.[4] Kasaragod was declared a district in the year 1984. Merger of Kasargod with Kerala has been a contentious issue as there is a sizeable population which speaks Kannada and Tulu which are the languages of Karnataka. Government of India formed the Mahajan Commission on 25 October 1966 to look into the issue along with a similar issue Karnataka had with Maharashtra over Belagaavi district.
